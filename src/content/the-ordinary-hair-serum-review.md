I've been using [The Ordinary Hair Serum](/go/the-ordinary-hair-serum) for three months, and honestly? It's not the miracle worker some people claim it is. But it's also not the complete waste of money I expected from a $9 serum.

The Ordinary's Multi-Peptide Serum for Hair Density is their attempt at creating an affordable alternative to expensive hair growth treatments. It combines peptides, caffeine, and plant extracts in a lightweight formula that's supposed to make your hair thicker and healthier over time.

## What's Actually in The Ordinary Hair Serum

The ingredient list reads like a chemistry textbook, but here's what matters:

**Peptides** (Acetyl Tetrapeptide-3, Triapeptide-1): These are the star players. They're supposed to stimulate hair follicles and improve circulation to your scalp.

**Caffeine**: Same stuff that wakes you up in the morning. In hair products, it's meant to block DHT (the hormone that causes hair loss) and increase blood flow.

**Procyanidin B-2**: A plant compound that some studies suggest can help with hair growth. The research is limited but promising.

**EGCG from green tea**: An antioxidant that might protect hair follicles from damage.

What's missing? Minoxidil, the only FDA-approved topical ingredient proven to regrow hair. That's a big deal we'll get to later.

## My Experience: 3 Months of Daily Use

I applied the serum every night to my scalp, focusing on areas where my hair was thinning. The texture is thin and watery, which I actually prefer over thick, sticky serums that make your hair look greasy.

The dropper bottle is practical but not elegant. You'll go through about one bottle per month with daily use, which The Ordinary doesn't make super clear on their packaging.

**Week 1-4**: Nothing dramatic. My scalp felt slightly tingly after application, which was reassuring but probably just the caffeine.

**Week 5-8**: I noticed less hair in my shower drain. Could've been seasonal, could've been the serum. Hard to tell.

**Week 9-12**: My hair definitely felt thicker when I ran my fingers through it. New growth? Maybe. But we're talking subtle changes, not a full head of new hair.

## How It Stacks Up Against the Competition

### The Ordinary vs Rogaine Minoxidil 5%

[Rogaine](/go/rogaine-minoxidil) is the gold standard because it actually works. Clinical studies prove it. The Ordinary's serum has promising ingredients but zero clinical trials backing its effectiveness.

**Rogaine pros**: FDA-approved, clinically proven, available in foam or liquid
**Rogaine cons**: Can cause scalp irritation, requires lifelong use, more expensive ($30-40/month)

**The Ordinary pros**: Gentler formula, cheaper ($9/bottle), nice ingredient list
**The Ordinary cons**: No proven efficacy, results are subtle at best

If you're dealing with serious hair loss, Rogaine wins. If you want something gentle for early thinning, The Ordinary might be worth trying.

### The Ordinary vs Nutrafol

[Nutrafol](/go/nutrafol) takes a completely different approach with oral supplements instead of topical application.

| Feature | The Ordinary | Nutrafol |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Method | Topical serum | Oral supplement |
| Price/month | ~$9 | ~$88 |
| Clinical studies | None | Multiple |
| Results timeline | 8-12 weeks | 3-6 months |
| Side effects | Rare | Possible digestive issues |

Nutrafol addresses hair loss from the inside out with vitamins, minerals, and adaptogens. It's expensive but has solid research behind it. The Ordinary is a fraction of the cost but with questionable results.

I'd probably try The Ordinary first, then upgrade to Nutrafol if you don't see improvement after three months.

### The Ordinary vs Vegamour GRO Serum

[Vegamour GRO Serum](/go/vegamour-gro) is The Ordinary's closest competitor in the peptide-based serum category.

**Vegamour** uses different peptides plus red clover and mung bean extracts. It costs about $58 for a month's supply, making it six times more expensive than The Ordinary.

I tested both side by side. Vegamour felt more luxurious and came in prettier packaging, but I honestly couldn't tell a significant difference in results. The Ordinary's formula seems just as effective for a fraction of the price.

If you're on a budget, stick with The Ordinary. If money isn't an issue and you want the premium experience, Vegamour might be worth the splurge.

## The Supplement Question: Biotin and Rosemary Oil

Many people pair hair serums with supplements like [Biotin 10000mcg](/go/biotin-10000mcg) or natural oils like [Rosemary Oil](/go/rosemary-oil).

**Biotin**: Only helps if you're actually deficient, which most people aren't. Save your money unless a blood test shows you need it.

**Rosemary Oil**: Some studies suggest it's as effective as 2% minoxidil. I mix a few drops with a carrier oil and massage it into my scalp twice a week. It's cheap and smells great.

Using The Ordinary serum with rosemary oil treatments actually makes sense. Different mechanisms, complementary approaches, minimal cost.

## Who Should (and Shouldn't) Use This

**Skip it if**:
