## What Rosemary Oil Actually Does for Hair Growth

Rosemary oil works by improving circulation to your scalp and blocking DHT (the hormone that shrinks hair follicles). A 2015 study found it performed as well as 2% minoxidil for androgenetic alopecia after six months of use.

I started using it because I was tired of the side effects from [Rogaine](/go/rogaine). The constant scalp irritation and that weird sticky residue that made my hair look greasy by noon. Rosemary oil promised a "natural" alternative, which honestly sounded like wellness marketing nonsense.

Turns out, the science is actually solid. Rosemary contains carnosic acid, which repairs nerve damage and improves circulation. Better blood flow means more nutrients reach your hair follicles. Simple concept, surprisingly effective execution.

### How I Used It (And What Actually Worked)

Most people dilute rosemary essential oil with a carrier oil like jojoba or coconut oil. I tried this for two months and saw minimal results. The breakthrough came when I switched to a pre-made rosemary oil blend specifically formulated for hair growth.

My routine:
- Apply to dry scalp 2-3 times per week
- Massage for 5 minutes (this part is crucial)
- Leave on for at least 2 hours, often overnight
- Wash out with regular shampoo

The massage matters more than I expected. Just slapping oil on your head and hoping for the best won't cut it. You need to work it into the scalp to boost circulation.
