## The Bottom Line Up Front

[Nutrafol](/go/nutrafol) is a premium hair growth supplement that costs $88 per month and targets hair thinning from the inside out. After six months of testing, I can say it works, but slowly and expensively. You'll see subtle improvements in hair thickness around month 3-4, but don't expect dramatic results.

If you're dealing with stress-related hair loss or nutrient deficiencies, Nutrafol might be worth the investment. If you're looking for faster results or have male pattern baldness, [Rogaine](/go/rogaine-minoxidil-5) will likely serve you better.

## What Nutrafol Actually Does

Nutrafol isn't a topical treatment like most hair growth products. It's a daily supplement that claims to address the root causes of hair thinning: stress, hormones, metabolism, and nutrition.

The core formula includes saw palmetto (blocks DHT), ashwagandha (reduces cortisol), marine collagen, biotin, and a bunch of other vitamins. They have different formulations for men, women, and postmenopausal women.

I tested the Women's formula for six months. The pills are large, beige capsules that you take four times daily with meals. Yes, four pills. Every day. That's 120 pills per month.

## My Experience: Month by Month

**Month 1-2:** Honestly? Nothing. My hair looked exactly the same. I did notice my nails growing faster, which makes sense given the biotin content.

**Month 3:** This is when I started seeing subtle changes. My part looked slightly less wide, and I had some baby hairs sprouting along my hairline. Not dramatic, but noticeable if you're looking for it.

**Month 4-6:** The improvements continued slowly. My ponytail felt thicker, and I was losing fewer hairs in the shower. My hairstylist even commented that my hair looked fuller.

But let's be clear: this isn't a miracle transformation. We're talking about going from thin to slightly-less-thin.

In my testing, Vegamour showed results faster (around 6-8 weeks) but wasn't as comprehensive. Nutrafol addresses internal factors that topical treatments can't touch.

## The Science Behind Nutrafol

Nutrafol's approach is actually smart. Instead of just throwing biotin at the problem, they target multiple pathways:

**DHT Blocking:** Saw palmetto and pumpkin seed oil inhibit the hormone that shrinks hair follicles.

**Stress Reduction:** Ashwagandha and rhodiola help lower cortisol, which can trigger hair loss.

**Nutrient Support:** Marine collagen, vitamins D and B-complex, and zinc provide building blocks for hair growth.

The research on individual ingredients is solid. Saw palmetto studies show 27% improvement in hair count. Ashwagandha reduces cortisol by up to 30%.

But here's the catch: there aren't any published studies on Nutrafol's specific formula. You're trusting that their combination works better than individual ingredients.

## Potential Side Effects

Nutrafol is generally well-tolerated, but I experienced:
- Mild nausea if taken on an empty stomach
- Slightly increased appetite
- Faster nail growth (not really a side effect, but worth noting)

Some users report breakouts during the first month as hormones adjust. The company says this is normal and temporary.
