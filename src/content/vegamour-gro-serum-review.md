# Vegamour GRO Serum Review: Does This $52 Hair Growth Serum Work?

I've been using [Vegamour GRO Serum](/go/vegamour-gro-serum) for three months, and I'm ready to give you the unfiltered truth about this popular hair growth treatment. At $52 for a one-month supply, it's not cheap, but it promises to deliver thicker, fuller hair using plant-based ingredients instead of harsh chemicals.

The short answer? It works, but not for everyone, and definitely not as dramatically as the Instagram ads suggest.

## What Is Vegamour GRO Serum?

Vegamour GRO Serum is a topical hair growth treatment that uses a blend of plant-based ingredients to promote hair growth and reduce shedding. The key active ingredients include red clover, mung bean, and nicotinamide (a form of vitamin B3). Unlike [Rogaine](/go/rogaine-minoxidil), it doesn't contain minoxidil, which means no potential side effects like scalp irritation or unwanted facial hair growth.

The serum comes in a sleek dropper bottle that looks more like a luxury skincare product than a medical treatment. You apply it directly to your scalp once daily, ideally at night, and don't rinse it out.

## Key Features and How It Works

### The Science Behind the Formula

Vegamour's approach focuses on extending the anagen (growth) phase of your hair cycle while reducing inflammation that can lead to hair loss. The red clover extract contains isoflavones that may help block DHT (dihydrotestosterone), the hormone responsible for pattern baldness.

Mung bean extract provides amino acids and peptides that supposedly strengthen hair follicles, while nicotinamide improves blood circulation to the scalp. It's not as clinically proven as minoxidil, but the research on these ingredients is promising.

### Application and User Experience

The dropper makes application easy, though I found it takes some practice to get the right amount. Too little and you're not covering your problem areas effectively. Too much and your hair looks greasy the next morning.

The serum has a slightly herbal smell that fades quickly. It absorbs well and doesn't leave residue, which is crucial since you're supposed to leave it on overnight. I never had issues with staining pillowcases or irritating my scalp.

## My 3-Month Experience: The Good and Bad

### What Worked

After about 6 weeks, I noticed less hair in my shower drain. Not dramatically less, but enough that I started paying attention. By month three, the baby hairs around my hairline were definitely more noticeable, and my hair felt thicker overall.

The texture improvement was actually more impressive than the growth. My hair felt stronger and looked shinier, probably due to the nourishing ingredients.

### What Didn't Work

The growth wasn't anywhere near what the before-and-after photos on their website suggest. I gained maybe 10-15% more density in my problem areas, not the dramatic transformation I was hoping for.

Also, I had to be religious about application. Miss a few days and any progress seemed to stall. This isn't a "use it occasionally" kind of product.

I actually used both together for a month and saw better results than either product alone. Nutrafol costs about $88 per month, so combining them gets expensive quickly.

### Vegamour vs Rogaine

This is the big comparison everyone wants to know about. [Rogaine](/go/rogaine-minoxidil) contains 5% minoxidil, which has decades of clinical research backing its effectiveness. It's also much cheaper at around $25-30 per month.

Rogaine definitely works better for significant hair loss, but it comes with potential side effects like scalp irritation, unwanted facial hair growth in women, and the need for permanent use (stop using it and you lose your gains).

Vegamour is gentler and uses natural ingredients, but it's less effective for advanced hair loss. If you're dealing with early-stage thinning and want to avoid chemicals, Vegamour makes sense. For serious hair loss, Rogaine is still the gold standard.

### Vegamour vs The Ordinary Hair Serum

[The Ordinary's Multi-Peptide Serum](/go/the-ordinary-hair-serum) costs just $18 and uses peptides and caffeine to stimulate hair growth. It's a budget-friendly option, but the formula isn't as sophisticated as Vegamour's.

I tried The Ordinary's serum for two months before switching to Vegamour. While it helped with hair texture, I didn't see any meaningful growth. The Ordinary is fine for maintenance, but if you want actual results, you'll need something stronger.

### Natural Alternatives: Rosemary Oil and Biotin

Before diving into expensive serums, many people try [rosemary oil](/go/rosemary-oil) or [high-dose biotin supplements](/go/biotin-10000mcg). 

Rosemary oil has some promising research showing it can be as effective as 2% minoxidil for androgenetic alopecia. At $10-15 for a bottle that lasts months, it's worth trying first. Just dilute it with a carrier oil to avoid scalp irritation.

Biotin supplements are popular but only help if you're actually deficient in biotin, which is rare. Most people get enough biotin from their diet, so these supplements are essentially expensive urine.

## The Science: What Studies Actually Show

Vegamour funded a clinical study showing their serum increased hair density by 52% after four months of use. That sounds impressive, but the study was small (only 60 participants) and not peer-reviewed. 

The individual ingredients have better research backing them. Red clover extract has shown promise in small studies for blocking DHT, and nicotinamide can improve scalp circulation. But there's no independent research on Vegamour's specific formula.

Compare this to minoxidil, which has been studied extensively in large, randomized controlled trials. The evidence for Vegamour is promising but limited.

## Tips for Getting the Best Results

If you decide to try Vegamour, here's what I learned works:

1. **Be consistent**: Use it every single night. Missing applications really does slow progress.

2. **Start with clean hair**: Apply to a clean, dry scalp for better absorption.

3. **Don't expect overnight results**: Give it at least 8-12 weeks before judging effectiveness.

4. **Take progress photos**: Hair growth is gradual, and photos help you see changes you might miss day-to-day.

5. **Consider combining treatments**: I saw better results using Vegamour with a good scalp massage and occasional rosemary oil treatments.

## Potential Side Effects and Warnings

Vegamour is generally well-tolerated, but I did experience some minor issues:

- Slight scalp tingling for the first week (normal and goes away)
- Occasional flaking if I used too much product
- Hair looked greasy if I applied too close to my hairline

The company says it's safe for color-treated hair and during pregnancy/breastfeeding, but I'd still check with your doctor first.

## Bottom Line

Vegamour GRO Serum works for mild to moderate hair thinning, but it's not a miracle cure. At $52 per month, it's expensive for what you get, especially compared to proven alternatives like minoxidil or budget options like rosemary oil. If you want a natural, gentle approach to hair growth and don't mind paying premium prices, it's worth trying. For serious hair loss or budget-conscious buyers, look elsewhere.
